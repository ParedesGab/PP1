Candidatus T. oneisti

The Symbiont coat

Candidatus Thiosymbion oneisti is a unique gammaproteobacterium with a cool job! Except for the head and the tail, it colonizes the surface of the nematode Laxus oneistus, stablishing a one-to-one ectosymbiosis.

This rod-shaped bacterium has a superpowerful metabolism and a big genome (4.35 Mb) to match!

With the help of its nematode “ride,” it travels through sand layers, grabbing oxygen in the upper sand and sulfide down below to fuel itself and make energy. Together they form a tiny energy-hunting team!

How is Thiosymbion interacting with L. oneistus?

  • The below figure illustrates possible nutritional links between the partners in oxygen-rich upper layers and anoxic, sulfur-rich deeper layers of marine sediment (Paredes et al., 2021). We highlight key findings:
