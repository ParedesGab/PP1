A love story

Imagine wearing a winter coat in Caribbean heat?

The marine worm Laxus oneistus, does just that!

This tiny marine creature has a permanent symbiont bacterial "coat": Candidatus Thiosymbion oneisti, clinging to its surface - creating an everlasting relationship.

Discover below unique features of this oftenly unnoticed alliance, to fall a bit more in love with our amazing planet!

Extraordinary features!

Marine sand protagonists!

  • - Globally distributed in shallow-water marine sediments!
  • - Abundant in the Caribbean Sea.
  • - In the sand, the nematode moves up and down through its layers. Near the surface, it finds oxygen, but as it dives deeper, the oxygen fades away and sulfur takes over.
  • - The partnership enables these different environments to be conquered!
  • Fun fact: The nematode Laxus oneistus was discovered by three scientists, in the Carrie Bow Cay field station in Belize! (Ott et al., 1995).

One of a kind symbiosis!

  • - In nature, the vast majority of symbionts reside inside their hosts. This is called "endosymbiosis".
  • - On the other hand, "ectosymbioses" or the living on the surface" with few exceptions, is extremely rare!
  • -But, what makes this association even more puzzling is that they form a so-called monospecific ectosymbiosis. Discover what this is below!

  • Fun fact: Most animals host a bunch of different microbes, but Laxus oneistus is super selective, and it only teams up with the bacteria Candidatus Thiosymbion oneisti!
